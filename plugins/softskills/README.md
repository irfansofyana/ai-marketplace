# softskills

On-demand coaching for navigating tricky human dynamics at work.

## Skills

### office-politics-coach

A senior thinking partner for workplace situations where the technical answer is clear but the human answer isn't.

**Trigger phrases:**
- "office politics coach"
- "help me navigate this situation"
- "I need a thinking partner"
- "coach me through this"
- "how do I handle this at work"
- "I'm dealing with a difficult situation at work"

**What it does:**

The skill runs in three phases.

**Phase 1 (always first):** Asks sequential clarifying questions (targeting 3, minimum 2) before offering any advice. The questions surface the full situation, the organizational context, and what's actually at stake for you. No shortcuts — the questions always come first, one at a time.

**Phase 2 (after questions):** Produces a structured coaching response:

| Section | What it contains |
|---------|-----------------|
| **Reframe** | What's really happening beneath the surface — power dynamics, unmet needs, structural tensions. Not a summary of your situation back at you. |
| **Options** | 2-3 distinct paths forward, each with explicit trade-offs and what each option requires from you. Includes honest "this may not be fixable" assessments when warranted. |
| **Scripts** | Word-for-word language for the option you choose — with context on medium (in-person, Slack, email), timing, and fallbacks if it doesn't land. |

**Phase 3 (follow-through):** Stays in coaching mode after scripts are delivered. Handles follow-up when you report back on how it went, want to explore a different option, or when the situation evolves.

**Scenarios it handles well:**

- Manager bypassing you to go directly to your direct reports
- Peer conflicts and territory disputes over decisions or scope
- Being excluded from key decisions or meetings
- Direct reports undermining you, creating loyalty conflicts, or relitigating past decisions
- Difficult stakeholder conversations you've been putting off
- Feeling sidelined, underpowered, or undervalued in your role
- Navigating political dynamics when you have informal but not formal authority
- Cross-functional conflicts with no shared manager
- Situations where the honest answer might be "this isn't fixable"

**What it won't coach around:**

Harassment, discrimination, retaliation, or situations requiring HR/legal intervention. If the situation crosses that line, the skill will name it directly and redirect you to appropriate resources.

## Philosophy

Most workplace advice tools jump to recommendations too fast. The coaching value comes from understanding the situation before prescribing — specifically the organizational context, the other person's motivations, and what you're actually trying to achieve. This skill enforces that discipline: questions first, every time.

Scripts are written to sound like a real person who has thought carefully about what they want to say — not corporate-speak, not conflict-avoidant hedging. Direct and honest, ready to use in the moment.

When a situation isn't fixable, the skill says so. Coaching someone into trying harder in a broken system isn't coaching.
